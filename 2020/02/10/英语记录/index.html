    unit 3 I am doing (present continuous)

    something is happening now

    unit 4 are you doing? (present continuous questions)

    is/are + subject + -ing

    unit 5 I do/work/like etc.(present simple)

    We use the present simple for things that are true in general,or for things that happen somethings or all the time.


    always/never/often/sometimes/usually + present simple

    unit 6 I don’t … (present simple negative)

    The present simple negative is don’t/doesn’t + verb

    1. I drink tea but I don’t drink coffee.
    2. Jack drink coffee but he does’t drink tea.

    We use don’t/doesn’t + infititive (don’t like / dosen’t speak / doesn’t do etc.)
    eg: Sandra speaks Spanish but she doesn’t speak Italian.

    unit 7 Do you …? (present simple questions)

    We use do/does in present simple questions:
    do/dose + subject + infinitive

    unit 8 I am doing and I do (present continuous and present simple)

    Use the presimple only with these verb(I want / do you like? etc.):

    • I’m tired. I want to go home. (not ‘ I’m wanting ’)
    • Do you know that girl? Yes,but I don’t remember her name.
    • I don’t understand. What do you mean?

    Some verbs are irregular (= not regular). The past simple is not -ed.

    unit 15 I have done (present prefect 1)

    has cleaned / have gone etc. is the present perfect(have + past participle)


    We use the present perfect for an action in the past with a result now.

    unit 16 I have just … I’ve already … I haven’t … yet (present perfect 2)

    just = a short time ago (I’ve just …)
    already = before you expected / before I expected (I’ve already …)
    yet = untill now (I haven’t … yet / Have you … yet?)
    You can use yet in negative sentences and question. Yet is usually at the end.

    unit 17 Have you ever … ? (present perfect 3)

    We use the present prefect(have been / have had / have played etc.) when we talk about a time from the past until now.

    We use for + a period of time (three days / two years etc.)
    We use since + the start of the period (Monday / 9’oclock etc.)
    ago = before now:
    We use ago with the past (started/did/had/was etc.)
